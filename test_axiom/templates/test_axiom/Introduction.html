This activity is part of a study in decision-making. You will be asked to do three experiments.

In these experiments, we are going to simulate several envelopes. Let's call them "virtual envelopes". In reality, an envelope can prevent you from changing the notes sealed inside of it. The same goes for our experiments. We "seal" some information by using an online AES Encryptor, so the "virtual envelopes" are actually some pieces of encrypted messages, which look like: "U2FsdGVkX1+8FpVab/1AAEADz4ap/LEb54cczFNKB+w=". And then, we cannot change your envelope content during the course of the experiment. Now please let us explain more details.

We create the "virtual envelopes" using the following three steps:

  • Step 1: We go to a third party website named AES Encryptor: https://www.browserling.com/tools/aes-encrypt.
  • Step 2: Before each experiment, we encrypt 21 pieces of information with a password. These pieces of information are different and used to decide your payment.
  • Step 3: For each experiment, the AES Encryptor gives us 21 encrypted messages, for example: "U2FsdGVkX1+8FpVab/1AAEADz4ap/LEb54cczFNKB+w=". And these messages are called “virtual envelopes”.

    • During one experiment, you will need to choose one of the envelopes and answer one related question. Then after the experiment, we will give you a password to "open" your envelope on that third party website.

    Please note: you can only open your envelope with a right password. We set three different passwords for three experiments, so do not use the password from (say) experiment 1 to open the envelope you get in experiment 2. Otherwise, you will end up with a garbled message.
